COURTLY

courtly.com ↗

Courtly is a web app designed to simplify the process of booking tennis, badminton, and pickleball courts. The project focuses on clean UX flows, real-time calendar availability, and a user-friendly interface optimized for mobile and desktop use.

Tools

Cheers to breaking conventions
embracing flavor

Challenge

Users often faced frustration when trying to find and reserve courts due to scattered systems, outdated platforms, or a lack of real-time availability. The challenge was to design a unified and intuitive platform that makes the booking process seamless and accessible to everyone.

01 - Understanding User Pain Points

CI conducted informal interviews with recreational players and analyzed similar booking platforms to identify what worked and what didn’t. The key insight was that users wanted something fast, modern, and clear—with no unnecessary steps or clutter.

02 - Visual & Strategic Repositioning

To make the brand feel more current and authentic, I took a holistic approach to the design process. This included aligning with modern visual trends, simplifying messaging, and tailoring the experience to match the expectations of a dynamic and diverse audience. The result was a fresh, cohesive brand and interface that elevates the entire booking journey.

Process
📌

01/ We did some intense market research .

I followed a user-centered approach throughout the design. Starting with user flows and wireframes sketched in Figma, I laid the foundation for a clear and intuitive layout. The interface was designed to be fully responsive, with bold visuals and straightforward navigation that works across devices. I added hand-drawn touches using Procreate to give the design a more personal and playful feel. To demonstrate how interactions would work in real life, I created animated micro-interactions in After Effects, showcasing smooth transitions between states.

Accessibility was a priority from the beginning—I made sure to consider color contrast, touch-friendly elements, and clear call-to-actions. The final design includes location-based court search, a clean view of available court types, a simple three-step booking process, and immediate visual feedback upon booking confirmation.

Result

The final prototype received positive feedback for its clean layout and simple user flow. Testers found the booking process intuitive and appreciated the visual clarity across devices. This project helped me grow my skills in responsive design, prototyping, and motion interaction. The branding and interface now reflect a clear and cohesive identity—one that feels friendly, modern, and easy to trust. Overall, Courtly positions itself as a more user-focused and accessible option compared to existing platforms.
